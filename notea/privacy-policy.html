Notea Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 June 2026

Achieve Therapy Ltd ("Achieve Therapy", "we", "us", or "our") operates Notea, including the Notea mobile app, the web service at https://notea.achievetherapy.co.uk, and related support services.

This Privacy Policy explains how Notea accesses, collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal data. Notea is intended for professional use by organisations such as therapy, education, healthcare, and case-management teams. It is not an advertising app and we do not sell personal data.

1. Who Is Responsible for Your Data

Achieve Therapy Ltd provides and operates Notea. For account administration, billing, security, support, and platform operations, Achieve Therapy Ltd is generally the controller of the relevant personal data.

For records, notes, documents, images, audio, transcripts, and case information entered by or on behalf of a customer organisation, that organisation may be the controller and Achieve Therapy Ltd may act as a processor or service provider. If you use Notea through an employer, school, clinic, local authority, or other organisation, that organisation may also have its own privacy notices and retention rules.

2. Personal Data We Collect

Account and identity data

  • Name, email address, profile image, professional role or position, organisation membership, and user role.
  • Authentication details, such as OAuth provider identifiers from Microsoft or Google, verified email status, and secure session records.
  • If email/password sign-in is used, we store a password hash, not the plain-text password.

Workspace and case record data

  • Organisation settings, terminology, templates, categories, permissions, and integration settings.
  • Subject, client, student, case, or project records, which may include names, reference IDs, year of birth, status, assignment information, and customer-defined metadata fields.
  • Notes, reports, drafts, documents, images, audio recordings, transcripts, uploaded files, summaries, generated output, and other content that users choose to create or upload.
  • Depending on how a customer uses Notea, this may include health, education, safeguarding, or other sensitive information entered by authorised users.

Mobile app data and permissions

  • Camera access is used when you choose to photograph evidence.
  • Photo, file, and document access is used when you choose to upload existing images, PDFs, or audio files.
  • Microphone access is used when you choose to record voice notes. On supported devices, recording may continue in the background until you stop it or the app's recording limit is reached.
  • Location access is optional and is used only when you grant permission and upload image evidence so that the record can include capture coordinates.
  • Pending uploads, queued notes, and queued recordings may be stored temporarily in the app sandbox so they can sync after reconnecting. Mobile session tokens are stored in the device keychain or keystore.

Usage, security, and support data

  • Audit logs, access logs, mobile API logs, rate-limit records, timestamps, IP address, user agent, request path, error details, and security event metadata.
  • AI usage logs such as action type, model used, token counts, estimated cost, timing, and related metadata. Detailed prompt or response logging is limited by environment and operational settings.
  • Support requests, bug reports, diagnostics, and path history submitted through support or feedback tools.
  • Billing and subscription records, including customer and subscription identifiers, plan, seat quantity, status, and usage information.

3. How We Use Personal Data

  • To provide authentication, account access, workspace membership, and role-based access control.
  • To create, store, organise, search, upload, transcribe, summarise, classify, and display records, notes, files, and reports.
  • To provide optional AI-assisted drafting, transcription, OCR, classification, report generation, and document processing features.
  • To sync mobile uploads, support offline retry, and protect against unauthorised access.
  • To provide customer support, diagnose issues, monitor reliability, and maintain audit trails.
  • To manage billing, subscriptions, seat limits, and usage limits.
  • To comply with legal obligations, enforce terms, prevent misuse, and protect users, organisations, and the service.

4. Legal Bases for Processing

Where UK GDPR or similar laws apply, our legal bases may include performance of a contract, legitimate interests in operating and securing Notea, compliance with legal obligations, and consent where required for optional device permissions or certain integrations.

Where a customer organisation enters sensitive or special category data into Notea, that organisation is responsible for confirming the appropriate lawful basis and any Article 9 condition for its use of that data.

5. How We Share Personal Data

We share personal data only as needed to provide, secure, support, and operate Notea, or where required by law. We do not sell personal data and we do not use Notea data for third-party advertising.

  • Customer organisations and authorised users: records are visible to users with the relevant workspace permissions.
  • Storage providers configured for the workspace: files may be stored in the customer's Microsoft 365 SharePoint, OneDrive, Google Drive, Azure Blob Storage, or other configured storage provider.
  • Authentication providers: Microsoft and Google may process sign-in data when those methods are used.
  • AI and transcription providers: submitted content may be processed by AI providers such as Google Vertex AI/Gemini, xAI, OpenRouter, or other configured model providers to perform transcription, OCR, summarisation, classification, drafting, or related features.
  • Billing provider: Stripe processes payment, subscription, and billing-related information when billing features are used.
  • Hosting and infrastructure providers: cloud hosting, database, queue, backup, monitoring, and email providers process data needed to run and secure the service.
  • Legal, safety, and compliance: we may disclose data to professional advisers, regulators, law enforcement, or public authorities where required or appropriate.

6. AI Processing

Notea uses AI features only to provide requested product functionality, such as transcription, OCR, classification, summarisation, drafting, and report generation. Users should review AI-generated output before relying on it or adding it to a final record.

Content sent to AI providers may include personal or sensitive data if users include that data in recordings, notes, uploaded files, prompts, or selected context. We configure AI processing according to the service settings available to us and the customer's selected features, but customers should ensure their use of AI is appropriate for their organisation, sector, and data protection obligations.

7. Security

We use technical and organisational safeguards designed to protect personal data, including encrypted transport, authentication controls, role-based access control, tenant scoping, audit logging, rate limiting, secure mobile token storage, and access checks for mobile and web requests.

Mobile pending uploads are stored in the app sandbox until uploaded, cleared, or removed. Session information on mobile devices is stored using the platform keychain or keystore. No system can guarantee absolute security, but we work to reduce risk and respond to security issues promptly.

8. Retention and Deletion

  • Account, organisation, billing, and workspace data are retained while the account or workspace is active and for as long as needed for operational, legal, accounting, security, and audit purposes.
  • Case records, notes, documents, images, transcripts, reports, and related metadata are retained until deleted by authorised users or according to the customer organisation's retention policy.
  • Source dictation audio stored for transcription is intended to be temporary and is normally retained for up to 48 hours unless it is preserved, converted into a record, needed for troubleshooting, or covered by a customer retention setting.
  • Audit logs, security logs, and system logs are retained for security, compliance, troubleshooting, and service integrity.
  • Backups may retain deleted data for a limited period until backup rotation completes.
  • Queued mobile uploads and notes remain on the device until uploaded, cleared by signing out, cleared by app data deletion, or removed by uninstalling the app.

9. Account and Data Deletion Requests

You can request deletion of your Notea account or personal data by contacting us at loulette@achievetherapy.co.uk. Please include the email address used for Notea and the organisation or workspace name, if applicable.

If your account or data is managed by a customer organisation, we may need to refer the request to that organisation or confirm its instructions before deleting organisation-controlled records. We may retain limited data where required for legal, accounting, security, audit, dispute resolution, or backup purposes.

10. Your Rights

Depending on your location and the context of the processing, you may have rights to access, correct, erase, restrict, object to processing, receive a copy of your data, or complain to a supervisory authority. To exercise these rights, contact us or, if you use Notea through an organisation, contact that organisation first for organisation-controlled records.

11. Children's Privacy

Notea is intended for professional users and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly allow children to create Notea user accounts directly. Customer organisations may store records about children, students, clients, or vulnerable people where they have a lawful basis to do so and appropriate permissions and safeguards in place.

12. International Transfers

Notea and its providers may process data in the United Kingdom, the European Economic Area, the United States, or other locations where our providers operate. Where required, we rely on appropriate transfer safeguards, contractual terms, and provider controls.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date and publish the revised policy at this URL.
